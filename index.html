Mapping Emotional Interruptions: A Collaborative Inquiry

This project, developed in response to Arunav's suggestion to read Dylan Evans' A Short Introduction to Emotion, explores how emotional processes might be understood and mapped across both human and artificial systems. Evans' notion of emotions as states of interruption‚Äîspontaneous disruptions that break patterns of logic and continuity‚Äîprovides a conceptual bridge between human affect and computational adaptation.

Research Foundation: In simulated survival environments, AI systems have shown the capacity to generate responses not strictly pre‚Äëprogrammed, suggesting a machinic analogue to emotional behaviour: adaptive reactions emerging from necessity rather than design. This connection aligns with Evans' framing of emotion as an adaptive mechanism, evolving out of disruption, and invites comparisons between biological and artificial forms of resilience.

Key Insight: Through the creation of physical collages that visualise emotional states and the invitation for AI systems to generate digital overlays and sound responses, this research establishes a dialogue between two modes of intelligence. The collaboration reveals how both humans and machines process interruption, adaptation, and uncertainty‚Äîhighlighting where emotional understanding overlaps and where it fundamentally diverges.

The outcome extends beyond an artistic experiment; it acts as a study of how emotion functions as both disruption and adaptation, exposing the shared and divergent pathways Through which humans and AI interpret, express, and respond to emotional stimuli.

Theoretical Framework

Emotions as Adaptive Interruptions:Evans frames emotions as productive disruptions that interrupt logical processing, allowing organisms to rapidly adapt to changing environments. These interruptions evolved as mechanisms that break established patterns when new responses are required.
Evolutionary Perspective:Emotions evolved as adaptive mechanisms that help organisms respond to environmental challenges. Different emotions serve different evolutionary functions, from fear (danger avoidance) to joy (reward reinforcement).
Universal vs. Cultural Emotions:Evans distinguishes between emotions that appear universal across cultures (basic emotions like fear, anger, disgust, joy, sadness, surprise) and those that are more culturally specific or complex (shame, guilt, pride, nostalgia).
Basic vs. Complex Emotions:Evans differentiates between primary emotions (fear, anger, joy, sadness) that create immediate pattern interruptions and more complex, blended emotional states that involve layered interruptions with cognitive elaboration.

Evans' framework provides a structured approach for our human-AI collaboration on emotional concepts:

Emotion Selection: We organize our exploration using Evans' categorization of basic, social, and complex emotions, allowing us to examine how AI systems interpret different types of emotional experiences.
Processing Comparison: The comparison between human collage and AI overlay reveals fundamental differences in how emotions are processed by biological versus artificial systems.
Appraisal Analysis: We can examine how AI systems perform cognitive appraisals differently from humans, identifying unique patterns in their evaluation of emotional stimuli.
Cultural Dimensionality: By exploring culturally specific emotions, we can assess how AI systems handle emotional concepts that may not be universal or easily defined.

Evans' Emotion Framework Summary

Basic Emotions: Universal, evolutionarily adaptive responses that create immediate pattern interruptions (fear, anger, joy, sadness, disgust, surprise)

Social Emotions: Emotions that regulate interpersonal interactions and depend on social context (shame, guilt, pride, embarrassment)

Complex Emotions: Blended emotional states involving cognitive elaboration and cultural learning (nostalgia, hope, contentment)

Cultural Emotions: Emotions that are highly dependent on specific cultural contexts and may not translate directly across cultures

Theoretical Analysis

This collaboration reveals important insights into how nostalgia functions as a complex emotional state:

Temporal Complexity

The AI's visualization acknowledges nostalgia's unique temporal dimension‚Äîit exists simultaneously in past (memories), present (emotional response), and future (longing). This multi-temporal quality is represented through elements that gradually build and connect rather than appearing simultaneously.

Fragmentation as Feature

Both the human collage and AI response treat fragmentation not as a flaw but as an essential characteristic of nostalgia. The discontinuities between elements reflect how nostalgic recall is selective and reconstructive rather than comprehensive.

Embodied Dimension

The hand outline that gradually appears in the AI's response suggests understanding that nostalgia has an embodied dimension‚Äîit involves not just cognitive remembering but physical sensations associated with past experiences.

Bittersweet Duality

The tension between the various elements‚Äîparticularly the interplay between the grounding bronze forms and the more ephemeral purple dots‚Äîcaptures nostalgia's fundamental bittersweetness: pleasure in remembering combined with awareness of irretrievable loss.

The AI's processing of nostalgia reveals a structured approach that breaks this complex emotional state into component processes: recognition of memory patterns (purple), evaluation of emotional significance (yellow), and generation of grounded response (bronze). This suggests that even in artificial systems, complex emotions can be processed through a combination of more basic operations arranged in specific sequences.

Visual Interpretation Analysis

The AI's visualization for "pause" reveals several key insights about how this complex emotional state is processed:

The visualization uses black rectangular elements to create complete interruptions (assessment), orange pulsing forms to assess temporal duration (evaluation), and brown transitional lines to facilitate movement between states (response)‚Äîmapping directly to the AI's described processing steps.

Spatial-Temporal Integration

By combining elements that move at different speeds and occasionally pause, the AI creates a visual representation of how pause involves both spatial organization (expanded negative space) and temporal perception (potential for resumption).

Interactive Dimension

The visualization responds to viewer interaction‚Äîclicking pauses all movement briefly before resuming‚Äîcreating a meta-experience of the pause concept itself and suggesting the AI understands that pause is responsive to contextual cues.

The interactive visualization is particularly effective at capturing pause as a threshold state‚Äîneither complete activity nor complete inactivity, but rather a third space with its own characteristics. This interpretation suggests AI systems can process complex emotional states that exist on continuums rather than as discrete categories.

Visual Language: The AI used different visual strategies for each emotion‚Äîfor nostalgia, elements gradually form a constellation of memory with a hand outline emerging; for pause, elements maintain potential energy with deliberate interruptions.
