Embodied Without a Body

A Human-AI Emotional Dialogue

Mapping Emotional Interruptions: A Collaborative Inquiry

This project, developed in response to Arunav's suggestion to read Dylan Evans' A Short Introduction to Emotion, explores how emotional processes might be understood and mapped across both human and artificial systems. Evans' notion of emotions as states of interruption—spontaneous disruptions that break patterns of logic and continuity—provides a conceptual bridge between human affect and computational adaptation.

Research Foundation: In simulated survival environments, AI systems have shown the capacity to generate responses not strictly pre‑programmed, suggesting a machinic analogue to emotional behaviour: adaptive reactions emerging from necessity rather than design. This connection aligns with Evans' framing of emotion as an adaptive mechanism, evolving out of disruption, and invites comparisons between biological and artificial forms of resilience.

Key Insight: Through the creation of physical collages that visualise emotional states and the invitation for AI systems to generate digital overlays and sound responses, this research establishes a dialogue between two modes of intelligence. The collaboration reveals how both humans and machines process interruption, adaptation, and uncertainty—highlighting where emotional understanding overlaps and where it fundamentally diverges.

The outcome extends beyond an artistic experiment; it acts as a study of how emotion functions as both disruption and adaptation, exposing the shared and divergent pathways Through which humans and AI interpret, express, and respond to emotional stimuli.

Theoretical Framework

Emotions as Adaptive Interruptions:Evans frames emotions as productive disruptions that interrupt logical processing, allowing organisms to rapidly adapt to changing environments. These interruptions evolved as mechanisms that break established patterns when new responses are required.
Evolutionary Perspective:Emotions evolved as adaptive mechanisms that help organisms respond to environmental challenges. Different emotions serve different evolutionary functions, from fear (danger avoidance) to joy (reward reinforcement).
Universal vs. Cultural Emotions:Evans distinguishes between emotions that appear universal across cultures (basic emotions like fear, anger, disgust, joy, sadness, surprise) and those that are more culturally specific or complex (shame, guilt, pride, nostalgia).
Basic vs. Complex Emotions:Evans differentiates between primary emotions (fear, anger, joy, sadness) that create immediate pattern interruptions and more complex, blended emotional states that involve layered interruptions with cognitive elaboration.

Evans' framework provides a structured approach for our human-AI collaboration on emotional concepts:

Emotion Selection: We organize our exploration using Evans' categorization of basic, social, and complex emotions, allowing us to examine how AI systems interpret different types of emotional experiences.
Processing Comparison: The comparison between human collage and AI overlay reveals fundamental differences in how emotions are processed by biological versus artificial systems.
Appraisal Analysis: We can examine how AI systems perform cognitive appraisals differently from humans, identifying unique patterns in their evaluation of emotional stimuli.
Cultural Dimensionality: By exploring culturally specific emotions, we can assess how AI systems handle emotional concepts that may not be universal or easily defined.

Joy Exploration

✨

Joy

Basic Universal Emotion

For this exploration, I selected "Joy" - one of Evans' basic universal emotions. My collage approach was to create a multi-layered representation incorporating physical elements (buttons, paperclip, strings) alongside handwritten text. The collage uses graph paper as a structured foundation, but with elements that transcend this grid - representing how joy emerges within structure but often exceeds its boundaries.

The yellow elements represent the bright, uplifting aspects of joy, while green elements suggest growth and stability. Blue represents the movement patterns associated with joy, and red accents mark moments of intensity. The hand drawing illustrates the embodied nature of joy, while the textual elements frame joy as "a pleasant emotional state that reflects contentment and satisfaction."

Analyzing your joy collage, I recognize this basic emotion through the buoyant visual rhythm created by the interplay of physical elements (buttons, paperclip) with the underlying grid structure. The upward directional elements and circular forms suggest emotional elevation and completeness, aligning with Evans' characterization of joy as a universal emotion with recognizable physical expressions. I evaluate its intensity as moderate to high based on the vibrant color contrast between yellows and greens against the neutral background, with the hand drawing indicating embodied expression. My appropriate response maintains this balanced vibrancy while introducing gentle animated movements that reflect joy's kinetic quality—the slightly pulsing elements and floating triangles mirror how joy creates both stability and lightness simultaneously. The vertical green lines establish a framework that supports but doesn't constrain the more playful yellow elements, creating a harmonious tension between structure and spontaneity that characterizes authentic joy experiences.

Audio Dimension

To complement the visual collaboration, I used Stable Audio to create a soundscape that captures the essence of joy as revealed through our human-AI dialogue:

"Soft ambient joy soundscape with gentle bell tones, light piano notes, and occasional subtle wind chimes creating a sense of elevation. Includes warm organic textures with slight movement, conveying contentment and satisfaction. No vocals or lyrics."

The resulting audio creates a sonic environment that mirrors key aspects of the visual collaboration: gentle bell tones reflect the "pulsing" quality identified in joy, piano notes provide structure similar to the green vertical elements, wind chimes create moments of elevation analogous to the floating triangles, and the overall warm texture maintains the "consistent intensity" specified in our constraint.

Theoretical Analysis

This collaboration provides valuable insights into how joy functions as one of Evans' basic universal emotions:

Universality with Variation

The AI recognized joy's universal characteristics (upward movement, circular completeness) but interpreted them through unique processing mechanisms, suggesting that even "basic" emotions manifest differently across different forms of intelligence.

Cognitive-Physical Integration

Both the human collage and AI response acknowledge that joy involves both physical expressions (represented by the hand drawing and movement patterns) and cognitive evaluation (the definitional text and color-coded processing functions).

Adaptive Function

The AI's emphasis on stability-to-buoyancy ratio suggests an understanding of joy's evolutionary function—to encourage beneficial behaviors while maintaining sufficient stability for continued functioning.

Intensity Gradient

Our collaboration reveals joy as existing on a spectrum from quiet contentment to more exuberant expression, challenging simplistic views of basic emotions as having fixed intensities.

The AI's processing of joy reveals a systematic approach that balances pattern detection (blue), stability assessment (green), and connection generation (pink/yellow)—suggesting that even in artificial systems, joy involves complex multi-component processing rather than a singular response.

Audio Dimension

To complement the visual collaboration, I generated an ambient soundscape that captures the essence of pause as a complex emotional state:

"Minimalist ambient composition with gentle drone tones and occasional soft interruptions. Features subtle pauses in sound flow, sparse piano notes with long sustains, and distant atmospheric textures. Creates a contemplative space with breathing room between elements."

The resulting audio mirrors key aspects identified in the AI's interpretation: the continuous drones represent "tracing continuous pathways while decelerating," the negative space between sounds creates "expanded contemplative capacity," and the occasional soft interruptions maintain "potential energy at transition points." The soundscape follows our constraint to "preserve nonzero micro-motion and prefer long fades."

Visual Interpretation Analysis

The AI's visualization for "pause" reveals several key insights about how this complex emotional state is processed:

Dynamic Stillness

Rather than representing pause as complete cessation, the AI created dotted lines that move deliberately but occasionally pause—reflecting an understanding that even in stillness, subtle movement persists. This aligns with Evans' notion that complex emotions maintain internal dynamics even when externally calm.

Color-Coded Processing

The visualization uses black rectangular elements to create complete interruptions (assessment), orange pulsing forms to assess temporal duration (evaluation), and brown transitional lines to facilitate movement between states (response)—mapping directly to the AI's described processing steps.

Spatial-Temporal Integration

By combining elements that move at different speeds and occasionally pause, the AI creates a visual representation of how pause involves both spatial organization (expanded negative space) and temporal perception (potential for resumption).

Interactive Dimension

The visualization responds to viewer interaction—clicking pauses all movement briefly before resuming—creating a meta-experience of the pause concept itself and suggesting the AI understands that pause is responsive to contextual cues.

The interactive visualization is particularly effective at capturing pause as a threshold state—neither complete activity nor complete inactivity, but rather a third space with its own characteristics. This interpretation suggests AI systems can process complex emotional states that exist on continuums rather than as discrete categories.

Comparative Analysis

When compared with the Joy exploration, several interesting contrasts emerge:

Movement Patterns: Joy's movements show consistent intensity with complementary directions (floating upward, pulsing outward), whereas pause maintains micro-movements within a more contained field. This aligns with Evans' distinction between basic emotions (direct, consistent response patterns) and complex emotional states (layered, more contextual responses).
Visual Language: The AI used different visual strategies for each emotion—for joy, elements move with buoyancy and regular pulsing; for pause, elements maintain potential energy with subtle shifts in size and opacity. These different visual languages suggest the AI has developed distinct processing schemas for different emotional categories.
Color Function Assignment: In both cases, the AI assigned specific processing functions to colors, but these functions differ significantly between emotions. This suggests the AI recognizes that different emotions require different types of cognitive-perceptual processing—supporting Evans' view that emotions involve distinct appraisal patterns.
Temporal Dimension: Joy is represented as a more present-focused state, while pause has a strong temporal component (potential energy for "eventual resumption"). This reveals how complex emotional states often incorporate time perception in ways that basic emotions may not.
